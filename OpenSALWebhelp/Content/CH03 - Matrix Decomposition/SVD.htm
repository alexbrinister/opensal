Singular Value Decomposition

The singular value decomposition (SVD) of an m × n complex (or real) matrix A is a factorization of A into a pair of unitary (or, respectively, orthogonal) matrices U and V and a real diagonal matrix Σ so that A = U • Σ • VH.

Here:

  • U is m × m, V is n × n, and Σ = diag(σ0, σ1, …, σp–1) is m × n with σ0 ≥ σ1 ≥… ≥ σp–1 ≥ 0, where p = min{m, n}.
  • σi is the singular value of A.

The first p columns of U are the left singular vectors, and the first p columns of V are the right singular vectors. So:

A• vi = σi • ui for i = 0 to p–1

where vi denotes the ith column of V.

If A is real, the SVD reduces to:

A = U • Σ • VT

where U and V are real orthogonal matrices.

The largest singular value (σ0) and the smallest singular value (σp–1) have a special significance:

σ0 = ||A||2 is the matrix 2-norm

If A is non-singular, then:

σp–1 = 1/||A-1||2

Also, σ0/σp–1 = ||A||2• ||A-1||2 = k2(A), the 2-norm condition number.

If m ≥ n, and A = U• Σ • VH is an SVD, then the thin SVD is the decomposition:

A = U1 • Σ1 • VH

where U1 is an m × n matrix consisting of the first n columns of U, and Σ1 = diag(σ0, σ1, …, σn–1) is the n × n matrix consisting of the first n rows of Σ.

If all singular values below a certain threshold are treated as zeroes, then the number of singular values greater than or equal to this threshold is the numerical rank (r). An m × n (m ≥ n) matrix A is numerically rank-deficient if r < n. The SVD calculated from the first r rows of matrices U and V is called a truncated SVD of matrix A.

The zmat_svdx() SAL function performs the full, thin, or truncated SVD of a split complex m × n matrix, where m ≥ n. However, the diagonal matrix Σ is stored as a vector D, and the singular vectors are stored as rows rather, not columns. The rows of U are the left singular vectors, and the rows of V are the right singular vectors of matrix A. In this implementation, the SVD formula becomes:

A = UT• diag(D)· conj(V)

where conj(V) denotes the complex conjugate of matrix V, and diag(D) is the m × n diagonal matrix with ith diagonal element equal to D[i], i = 0 to n–1.
