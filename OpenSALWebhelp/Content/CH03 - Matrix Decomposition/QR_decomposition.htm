QR Decomposition

A thin QR decomposition, as produced by the modified Gram-Schmidt method (MGS), is a factorization of an m × n matrix A (with m ≥ n and rank(A) = n) into an m × n matrix Q with orthonormal columns multiplied by an n × n upper triangular matrix R. This contrasts with the full QR decomposition produced by other methods, where Q is a square m × m matrix with orthonormal columns (a unitary matrix) and R is an upper trapezoidal m × n matrix. If A has full column rank, then there is a unique thin QR factorization with all positive diagonal entries in R.

The mat_mgs_decx(), cmat_mgs_decx(), zmat_mgs_decx() SAL functions perform the thin MGS QR decomposition. These functions implement the reciprocal diagonal convention. For more information, see Reciprocal Diagonal Convention
